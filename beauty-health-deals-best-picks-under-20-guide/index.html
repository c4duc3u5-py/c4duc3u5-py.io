What to look for when you buy beauty & health items online (UK)

  • Ingredients and allergens: always read the full ingredient list. If a listing omits it, message the seller before buying.
  • Expiry and batch codes: check for manufacturing or expiry dates, especially on creams and oils. A sealed, brand-new item should show a clear batch code.
  • Seller ratings and returns: prefer sellers with 98%+ positive feedback and clear return policies. Low-cost health items should still have reasonable returns.
  • Sizing and fit: for mouthguards and physical accessories, verify measurements and whether the product is adjustable. Measure twice.
  • Patch tests and instructions: for topical acids or strong actives, test behind the ear or on the inner forearm for 24 hours. Follow dilution and use instructions exactly.
  • Storage and care: creams and oils last longer in cool, dark storage. Metal tools rinse with warm water and dry fully to avoid rust. Mouthguards should be rinsed and kept in ventilated cases.

Care tips and safety notes

  • Always use sunscreen after exfoliation products like peeling pads.
  • Keep herbal salves and natural oils away from direct heat to avoid separation. If separation occurs, a gentle warm-water bath will mix them; do not microwave.
  • For devices that go in the mouth, clean with toothbrush and mild soap, and air-dry overnight. Replace annually or as recommended.
  • For wart treatments, follow the timeline on the packaging; stopping early often leads to recurrence.
