What to look for when you buy beauty & health items on eBay UK

  • Check condition and sealing: New and sealed items preserve active ingredients. If a product is marked Brand new sealed, look for clear photos of the unopened packaging and batch code.
  • Inspect expiry dates and batch codes: For creams and serums, an in-date product is crucial. Ask the seller for expiry if it isn’t in the listing.
  • Read ingredients: Avoid products with known personal allergens. For intimate or sensitive-area ointments, avoid strong fragrances and known irritants.
  • Seller feedback and returns: Prefer sellers with 98%+ positive feedback and a returns policy. This matters for cosmetics and veterinary items.
  • Shipping and storage: Some treatments (natural oils, certain ointments) can degrade if shipped from very hot locations. Confirm seller location and shipping times.
  • Patch-test for new topicals: Apply a pea-sized amount to an inner forearm for 24 hours before broad use.
  • Veterinary products: Only treat pets after a professional diagnosis. Topicals may be fine for spot use but systemic infections need vet supervision.

When not to buy

  • If the listing lacks batch/expiry info for medicinal or topical products, avoid unless the seller can confirm date.
  • Avoid unbranded or no-ingredient listings for intimate or veterinary products.
  • If the seller has low feedback or no returns option for skin-sensitive items, look elsewhere.
