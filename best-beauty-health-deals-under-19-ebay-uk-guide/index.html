What to look for when buying beauty & health items under £19 on eBay UK

  • Seller reputation: Check feedback percentage and read recent reviews. High-volume sellers are more likely to handle returns professionally.
  • Exact quantities and sizes: Confirm ml, g, or oz (examples above: 130mL/60 sheets, 60g, 4oz) — small differences double up quickly on cost-per-use.
  • Ingredients and allergens: If you have allergies, ask the seller for ingredient lists before buying. Natural doesn’t always mean non-allergenic.
  • Seals and expiry dates: For creams and ointments, confirm items are sealed and check for expiry or manufacture date. A brand new listing should state sealed/brand new like the items above.
  • Returns and shipping: Look for tracked shipping and a reasonable returns window. If shipping cost is high, it can turn a good product into poor value.
  • Patch-test and sun care: For exfoliants and acids (e.g., lemon pads), start with patch tests and always use SPF after use.
  • Practical packaging: Tubes or jars? Tubes minimise contamination for creams; jars are fine for salves but use a clean spatula.

Quick care tips by product type

  • Oils: Store in a cool, dark place. Use within 6–12 months after opening.
  • Pads: Keep the container sealed and upright to prevent evaporation.
  • Salves: Keep lid tightly closed and use clean hands or a spatula to avoid contamination.
  • Devices/stands: Wipe aluminium or metal stands with a soft cloth; avoid abrasive cleaners.
