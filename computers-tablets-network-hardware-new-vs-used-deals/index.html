Used vs new: a short guide for computers, tablets & network hardware

  • Buy used when: the item is non-critical, has replaceable parts, or is an accessory (stands, speaker stands, many peripherals). Used saves most on items that don’t have firmware security concerns.
  • Buy new when: the device handles your network (routers) or stores personal data (phones, tablets), or when warranty and firmware updates are important. Routers often benefit from being new or certified refurbished to avoid hidden issues.
  • Inspect listings: photos, seller rating, tested status, return policy, and shipping terms are crucial. Free international shipping is a perk on several of these listings; factor delivery time into your decision.
