## What to look for when you buy cheap collectibles on eBay UK

- Condition specifics: Look beyond the broad label. “New (other)” can mean unused but repackaged, or a surplus single piece from a set. Ask for close-ups of rim, base, and gilt detail.  
- Gilt wear and plating loss: For gilt glass, examine the throat and base where wear first appears. For metal toast racks, check screw joints and underside for rust.  
- Provenance and branding: Brand names (Lurpak, Dema) and original boxes raise resale value. Always ask whether box is original and in what condition.  
- Sizing and capacity: Request exact dimensions (height, diameter) and capacities (ml) if you plan to use the glassware for serving. That prevents unpleasant surprises.  
- Shipping and combined postage: If you’re buying multiple listed “multibuy” items, ask the seller about combined postage to save money. Sellers often list single items but will combine postage on request.  
- Returns and buyer protection: Prefer sellers with 30-day returns and good feedback. Check estimated delivery times, especially for gifts.

## Care tips that really extend the life of gilt glass and metal kitchen collectibles

- Hand wash only for gilt glass. Use warm water, a mild detergent, and a soft sponge. Dry with a lint-free cloth to avoid water spots.  
- Avoid ultrasonic cleaners and abrasive pads on plated metal or gilt. These remove the decorative layers.  
- Store vintage glass in a stable temperature area. Sudden temperature changes can cause cracks. Use tissue paper between stacked items.  
- For metal toast racks, a quick polish with a non-abrasive metal cleaner will restore shine. Test on an inconspicuous spot first.
