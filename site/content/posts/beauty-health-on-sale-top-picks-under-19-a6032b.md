## What to look for when you buy beauty & health products online
- Ingredient transparency: Check for active concentrations (e.g., salicylic acid %) on exfoliants and wart treatments. If it’s missing, ask the seller before buying.  
- Patch test: Always patch-test serums, creams, and ointments for 24–48 hours to avoid reactions.  
- Sizing & expiry: Confirm volume (mL/oz) and check expiry/manufacture dates on the listing. Small bottles often have shorter shelf lives once opened.  
- Material & build: For physical items (shaker, stand), look for stainless steel or aluminium construction and measurements (this list shows a 9.5" stand and 4oz salve).  
- Returns & shipping: Verify seller returns and postage costs before purchasing — shipping is calculated at checkout on eBay UK listings.

## Quick buying tips and sizing guidance
- For eye creams, a 15mL tube lasts several months; budget £10–£30 depending on claims.  
- For body oils, apply to damp skin; 60mL is travel-sized while 100–200mL is better value for daily use.  
- For toner pads, start with every-other-day use; 60 sheets are typical for a one–two month course.  
- For sleep devices, don’t exceed recommended wear time and consult a clinician if you have existing jaw or dental issues.
