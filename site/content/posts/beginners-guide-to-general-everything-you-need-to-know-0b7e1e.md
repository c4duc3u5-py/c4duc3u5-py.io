## Step-by-step buying advice for general items on eBay UK

1. Inspect photos closely: zoom in for chips, cracks, missing parts or watermarks on box edges.
2. Read the condition text: “Brand New” usually means sealed; “Pre-owned” should list defects.
3. Ask the seller a question about dimensions and materials (e.g., cup capacity, cutlery length, kit scale).
4. Compare postage: cheaper item + high shipping can be worse than a slightly pricier local seller.
5. Check returns — model kits and fragile china are worth buying from sellers with at least 14-day returns.

## What to look for — specific checklist

- Materials: porcelain/fine bone china vs. stoneware; stainless steel grade (18/8 is better than 18/0).
- Finish: gold rims and printed designs need gentle care; look for confirmation in the listing.
- Included parts: kits should list sprues, instructions, and decals; bar kits should list specific tools.
- Measurements: capacity for cups (ml), length for cutlery (cm), scale for models (1:96 etc.).
- Photos: multiple angles, close-ups of any wear.

## Common mistakes to avoid

- Buying a “Brand New” kit without confirming included extras — some sellers omit decals or rigging.
- Overlooking shipping damage risk for fragile items — always ask how items are packaged.
- Assuming “gold rim” equals real gold — it’s often gold-coloured plating; care accordingly.
- Falling for low price on used items without photos — request close-ups before committing.

## Care and sizing tips

- Cups & saucers: handwash printed or gold-trim designs; store saucers flat to avoid chipping.
- Cutlery: avoid dishwashers for coloured handles; dry immediately to prevent spotting.
- Die-cast models: dust gently and avoid knocks; plastic parts can become brittle with age.
- Model kits: keep spare glue and cotton rigging thread; paint with enamel or acrylics specified on the instruction sheet.
