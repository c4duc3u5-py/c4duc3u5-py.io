## What to look for when you buy kitchen & dining items online
- Condition terms: Brand new, New (other), Pre-owned — "Brand new" and "Boxed" reduce inspection time, but always read photos for scuffs.  
- Material callouts: porcelain vs bone china vs stoneware; porcelain is thinner, bone china is lighter, stoneware is thicker and chip-resistant.  
- Measurements: capacity in ml for mugs and glasses; diameter/height in cm for saucers and racks. If missing, ask the seller.  
- Care labels: dishwasher-proof vs handwash only — this affects long-term finish and gilt/printed patterns.  
- Return policy and shipping cost: low-priced items (under £20) can become poor value with high postage.

## Practical care tips
- Handwash gilt, printed, or novelty items to preserve decoration.  
- For stainless cutlery, dry immediately to prevent spotting; use a soft cloth.  
- Store boxed or boxed-condition items in a cool, dry place to preserve paper boxes and prevent damp damage.  
- For vintage glassware, use mild detergent and soft brushes; avoid thermal shocks.

## Sizing and compatibility guidance
- Mugs typically hold 250–350ml; if you need a larger travel mug, look for explicit ml.  
- Cutlery sets labelled "6" usually mean six place settings; check whether forks/spoons include tablespoons or teaspoons.  
- For serving pieces (toast racks, racks, trays), measure your cabinet or shelf height — some decorative items are taller than they look in photos.

## Common mistakes to avoid when you buy kitchen & dining items
- Buying rare or vintage items without checking for chips or repairs. Photos should show edges and bases.  
- Assuming "new" means boxed — confirm if BNIB (brand new in box) or new without original packaging.  
- Overlooking shipping and import fees — a cheap item with expensive postage isn't a bargain.  
- Ignoring care instructions — gilt prints and novelty paints fade if put in a dishwasher.
