## What to look for when buying pre-owned controllers or VR gear

- Functional tests: Ask the seller if the controller has been tested with SteamVR and whether firmware is up to date. Functional tracking and button responsiveness are critical.
- Battery and charging: Confirm battery health and that the included USB-C cable charges the unit. A cable is included with the Valve Index Left Knuckle listing, which saves you buying one separately.
- Strap integrity and sensors: Check straps, velcro, and any silicone or foam that contacts your hands. These wear over time; clear photos help.
- Compatibility notes: Knuckle controllers require SteamVR tracking (Lighthouse/base stations). If you don’t own those, factor in additional hardware costs.
- Return policy and shipping: Look for sellers offering returns or clear warranty terms. Check delivery speed and postage costs for UK addresses.

## Care tips after purchase

For the Valve Index controller:
- Store in a padded case or foam-lined box to protect the tracking ring and sensors.
- Wipe exterior ABS plastic with a microfiber cloth and isopropyl wipe (lightly damp, not wet).
- Avoid long exposure to direct sunlight which can warp straps or degrade adhesives.
- Keep firmware updated through SteamVR to maintain button mapping and tracking performance.

For the board game:
- Open the sealed box carefully at the corners to preserve resale value if you change your mind.
- Use card sleeves (standard eurogame size) to protect high-use cards.
- Store the box upright in a cool, dry place to prevent warping or moisture damage.

## Common mistakes to avoid when you buy video games or hardware

- Buying used hardware without asking for test confirmation. Always request a video or seller statement that the device powers on and pairs.
- Ignoring compatibility. A left Index knuckle controller is not a complete VR kit — it must pair with right controller and base stations for full functionality.
- Overlooking shipping costs. A cheap listing can become expensive if postage is high or international.
- Skipping the seller’s feedback history. Low feedback or recent negative reviews are red flags.
- Not accounting for extras. Headsets, batteries, or card sleeves add to the real cost of ownership.
