## What to look for when buying kitchen & dining items on eBay UK

- Condition and photos: New vs pre-owned matters. Look for close-ups of rims, handles, and any maker’s marks. Boxed item photos should show the box condition if packaging is important.
- Materials and care: Confirm if items are porcelain, bone china, ceramic, stainless steel or plated metal. Ask sellers whether pieces are dishwasher-safe or hand-wash only.
- Dimensions and capacity: For cups and mugs get cup capacity (ml) and saucer or plate diameters (cm). For cutlery, confirm lengths (forks ~20–22cm, spoons ~18–23cm).
- Metal finishes: For vintage plated pieces, check for wear, tarnish or plating loss. Avoid items with deep pitting unless you want them for restoration.
- Authenticity & branding: For branded items (Cath Kidston, Lurpak), verify logos, labels and packaging. Scans of tags or maker’s marks are helpful.
- Shipping & returns: Always review postage cost and whether the seller accepts returns. For fragile boxed items, pay attention to how the item is packaged — double boxing is a good sign.

## Quick care tips that save money
- Hand-wash patterned or gilded china to keep decoration bright.
- Use non-abrasive metal polish on plated toast racks — test a small area first.
- For novelty mugs with removable parts, remove plastic components before washing and air-dry thoroughly.
- Store cutlery flat in trays to avoid scratches and dents.
