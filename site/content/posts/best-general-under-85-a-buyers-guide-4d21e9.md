## What to look for when you buy general items online (practical buying tips)

- Condition and completeness: For model kits and electronics check whether the listing specifies “sealed”, “opened – never used”, or “pre-owned”. Missing parts (rigging, decals, chargers) are the biggest cost sink.
- Photos and packaging: Boxed items retain value. Request photos of seals, serial numbers or manuals if not present. For opened electronics, confirm battery health and included cables.
- Materials and care: Glass with gold rims, or colour-coated cutlery, often requires hand-washing. Stainless steel marked 18/8 means better corrosion resistance.
- Size and fit: For models and display stands, match measurements. If dimensions aren’t listed, ask the seller—wheelbase, cup capacity (600ml on that spray gun) and physical length matter.
- Compatibility: For gimbals, check that your phone model fits and that the app/firmware is supported on your device. Verify whether the seller provides original accessories.
- Shipping and returns: Shipping costs can negate a “cheap general” buy. Confirm return window and who pays for return postage if the item is not as described.
- Red flags: Blurry listing photos, vague descriptions (“works fine”) or missing serial numbers on electronics. For gold-trimmed glassware, look carefully for loss of gilt.

## Care and maintenance quick wins
- Store model kits in a dry, cool place; humidity can warp small plastic parts.
- Clean HVLP guns immediately after use—dry paint is a nightmare to remove.
- Hand-wash gold-rim glasses and avoid dishwashers.
- Keep gimbals charged and store without load on motors to preserve bearings.
