## Practical buying tips for kitchen & dining bargains
- Condition language: “Brand new”, “Brand new in box (BNIB)”, “New (other)”, “Pre-owned (Good/Very Good)” all have different implications. BNIB is best for gifting/resale; “New (other)” often means new with minor packaging faults.  
- Materials matter: stainless steel vs plated metal vs bone china each need different care. If plating is present, avoid dishwashers. Bone china and gilt trims prefer hand-washing.  
- Dimensions & capacity: mugs and cups vary (200–350ml common). If the listing lacks exact ml or mm, message the seller before buying.  
- Photos and returns: look for clear close-ups of scratches, stamps, or maker marks. Prefer listings with a returns policy — small items can be damaged in transit.  
- Seasonal relevance: boxed sets and floral teaware make good gifts heading into Mother’s Day or summer gatherings — quantities are often limited, so check stock sooner rather than later.

## Quick care & sizing cheat-sheet
- Ceramic mugs: hand-wash; check for microwave-safe label if you plan to heat drinks. Typical mug capacity: 300–350ml.  
- Fine china: hand-wash only; avoid sudden temperature changes. Cups often 150–220ml.  
- Metal flatware: if coated, hand-wash to avoid finish wear; stainless steel (18/10) is dishwasher-safe.  
- Vintage glassware with gilt: hand-wash, dry immediately, store upright with padding.
