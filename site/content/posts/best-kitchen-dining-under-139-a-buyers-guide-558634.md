## What to look for when buying kitchen & dining items online (UK buyers)

- Condition and provenance: For vintage or rare items (look for “boxed” or “BNIB”), boxed condition increases value. Ask for close-ups of hallmarks or maker’s marks, especially with 1970's or older pieces.  
- Materials matter: Stainless steel should be 18/10 for corrosion resistance. Bronze, brass or gold-plated items need handwashing and will patina — decide if you like that finish.  
- Dimensions and weight: Cutlery balance and mug capacity (in ml) determine user comfort. If a listing omits sizes, ask the seller before buying.  
- Dishwasher vs handwash: Many decorative or vintage items are not dishwasher-safe. Confirm care instructions to avoid ruining trims or coatings.  
- Shipping and returns: Check postage cost and estimated delivery on eBay.co.uk. For boxed, fragile items, insured tracked shipping is worth the extra cost.  
- Inspect for repairs: For pre-owned cutlery or brass sets ask about repairs, soldering, or replaced handles — these reduce value and durability.  
- Rarity cues: Marked limited editions or manufacturer labels boost collectability. For 1970’s pieces, seek documentation or dated packaging.

---

## Care tips that extend lifespan

- Store flatware separated: Avoid stacking mixed metals to prevent galvanic tarnishing. Use soft cloth rolls for boxed vintage cutlery.  
- Gentle cleaners: Use pH-neutral detergents. For bronze, use a paste of baking soda and lemon for occasional polishing, then oil lightly.  
- Regular inspection: Tighten screws on mugs with removable parts (like the Mr Potato Head mug) and replace damaged silicone or cork coasters promptly.
