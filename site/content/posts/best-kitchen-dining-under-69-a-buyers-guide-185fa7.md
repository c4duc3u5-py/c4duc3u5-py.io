## What to look for when you buy kitchen & dining items on eBay UK

- Condition and wording: “Brand new”, “New (other)” and “Pre-owned” have clear resale and usage implications. Boxed items often command higher prices; photos should show box condition.  
- Materials and markings: For porcelain/cups, look for maker’s marks or stamps on the base. For stainless cutlery, confirm 18/10 vs 18/0 if corrosion resistance matters.  
- Size and capacity: Check mug capacities (ml), cup diameters, saucer diameter, and glass height to ensure they fit cabinets and practical needs.  
- Dishwasher/microwave safety: Many vintage or gilt-trim items are not dishwasher/microwave safe. Always check listing notes or ask the seller.  
- Collectible checks: For rare 1970s pieces (Dema, Lurpak limited editions), request close photos of logos, wear, and box contents. Provenance or original sales tags raise value.  
- Returns and postage: Confirm the seller’s returns policy and postage costs — inexpensive items can become poor deals with high postage. Look for UK-based sellers to keep delivery fast and local.  
- Care and use guidance: Handwash gilt, avoid harsh detergents on decals, and flush tools like spray guns immediately after use with the correct solvent.
