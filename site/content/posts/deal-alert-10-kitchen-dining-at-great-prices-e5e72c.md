Buying tips — what to look for when you buy kitchen & dining items on eBay UK
### Condition and packaging
- For collectables (Lurpak toast rack, Cath Kidston set), boxed and “Brand new” or “New in Box” dramatically increases value. Inspect seller photos for box wear and ask for additional shots if unclear.
- For pre-owned cutlery and metal items, ask about plating wear, pitting or bending.

### Materials and care
- Bronze: expect patina and handwashing only. Avoid acidic long-term contact.
- Stainless steel: look for “18/10” or “18/8” markings for food-safe, corrosion-resistant flatware. These are usually dishwasher-safe but painted handles may not be.
- Ceramics and bone china: confirm microwave/dishwasher suitability if you’ll use them daily.

### Size and fit
- Mugs: standard capacity ranges 250–350ml; ask seller if not specified.
- Cutlery: consider handle length and weight — heavier pieces are often more comfortable and indicate better quality.

### Authenticity and marks
- For designer items (Cath Kidston) ask for tags, serial numbers or receipts where available.
- For metalwork (bronze set) ask for hallmarks or close-ups of the finish to confirm “solid bronze” rather than plated alloys.

### Shipping & returns
- Check postage cost, estimated delivery times and return policy before you buy. Seasonal demand can delay shipping — factor that in for gifts.

Soft urgency: many of these items are single-quantity listings or limited editions (collector’s toast rack, Cath Kidston boxed set) — if you like it, the seller’s stock often won’t last.
