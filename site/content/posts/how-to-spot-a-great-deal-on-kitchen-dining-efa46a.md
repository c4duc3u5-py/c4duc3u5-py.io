## What to look for when buying Kitchen & Dining items online (practical tips)
- Boxed/boxed photos: If the listing says "Boxed" or "New in Box", ask for a photo of the box intact. Boxed items maintain value and are easier to gift.
- Material callouts: Stainless steel, ceramic and metal are durable. For painted or novelty items, verify finish longevity.
- Function vs. display: Decide whether the item will be used daily (cutlery, mugs) or kept for display (limited editions, branded pieces).
- Confirm completeness: For novelty mugs with detachable parts (like the Mr Potato Head mug), ensure all pieces are present.
- Seller feedback: Prefer sellers with clear return policies and recent positive reviews for similar items.
- Shipping cost vs. item value: A £10 item with £8 postage may be a poor deal — check combined cost.

## Common mistakes to avoid
- Assuming boxed means mint — always ask for close-up photos of box corners and seals.
- Overlooking cleaning or maintenance requirements — metal polish, hand washing or regular descaling can be necessary.
- Ignoring dimensions — toast racks and mugs have standard sizes; if your shelves are shallow, check measurements.
- Buying duplicates from different sellers without checking total postage and dispatch time.

## Care and sizing guidance — short, usable tips
- Ceramic novelty mugs: hand washing recommended for detachable parts; avoid the microwave unless the seller confirms safety.
- Stainless steel cutlery: avoid prolonged soaking and use a soft cloth to maintain sheen.
- Metal toast racks: dry thoroughly after washing to avoid rust spots on older plated pieces.
- HVLP spray guns: always flush with solvent after use and store sealed to prevent drying in the nozzle.
