## What to look for when you buy kitchen & dining items on eBay UK

- Condition photos: insist on clear, close-up photos showing maker’s marks, joins, and any chips or scratches. For boxed items, request images of box seams and labels.  
- Material markings: look for "18/10" or "18/8" on stainless steel; "bronze" or patina descriptors for metals; “bone china” or “porcelain” for finer tea/coffee sets. These impact longevity and care.  
- Size and capacity: ask for measurements (handle length for cutlery, cup capacity in ml, glass height and diameter). These affect storage and how pieces fit your dishwasher or cabinets.  
- Returns and postage: confirm seller’s returns policy and exact postage cost to UK (especially for heavier bronze sets). Shipping can push a cheap item past your budget.

## Care tips that keep pieces looking new

- Metal cutlery: avoid extended soaking; dry immediately to prevent water spots. Polished bronze benefits from occasional brass/bronze polisher.  
- Ceramic and printed mugs: hand wash to preserve printed patterns; use soft sponges. If microwave-safe is not stated, don’t microwave.  
- Vintage gilt glass: never use dishwasher cycles; hand-wash with mild detergent and soft cloth.  
- Tools (spray gun): clean solvents through all channels after use and store in original box to protect seals.

## Common mistakes to avoid when you buy kitchen & dining pieces

- Buying blind on condition. Vague photos or "see pics" without close-ups often hide flaws.  
- Ignoring postage cost. Heavy boxed items (bronze sets, boxed crockery) can double the price once shipping is added.  
- Assuming dishwasher-safe. Many decorative or gilt-edged pieces are not dishwasher-safe despite modern trends.  
- Overlooking returns. If the seller has strict no-return terms, you take more risk on collectibles.
