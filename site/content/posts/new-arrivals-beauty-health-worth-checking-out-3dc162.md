## Buying tips: what to look for when you buy beauty & health items online UK
- Ingredients and allergens: Always read the full ingredient list on the eBay page, especially for creams and salves. Patch-test unknown botanical products for 24–48 hours.  
- Packaging size vs. use: Check grams or millilitres (e.g., 4oz = ~113g, 60ml bottles) so you know the value per use. Small jars are portable but can run out fast if used daily.  
- Pad frequency and pH for exfoliants: Toner pads with acids need clear frequency guidance; if the listing doesn’t state acid type or concentration, proceed cautiously and use less often.  
- Mouthguard fit and safety: Look for boil-and-bite instructions, or consult seller on sizing. Avoid overnight use if you have dental work or TMJ pain without professional advice.  
- Storage and care: Oils and actives last longer in a cool, dark place. Aluminium stands and stainless-steel barware should be hand-dried to avoid water marks and extend hinge life.  
- Seller shipping and returns: Confirm postage cost and estimated delivery on the listing; check returns policy if you’re concerned about fit, sensitivity or damage.
