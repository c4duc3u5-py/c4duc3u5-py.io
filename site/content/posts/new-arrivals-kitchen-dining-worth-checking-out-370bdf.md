## What to look for when you buy kitchen & dining items online (practical tips)
- Condition details: For vintage or pre-owned, insist on clear photos of edges, joins, and any hallmarks. Small dents or plating wear are common with toast racks and cutlery.
- Materials matter: Bronze needs handcare; stainless steel is low maintenance. Porcelain varies by glaze — check for crazing or hairline cracks shown in images.
- Dimensions and capacity: Verify cup volume and utensil length if you need forks to fit narrow drawer organisers or mugs to fit under your kettle spout.
- Function vs. display: Decorative pieces (limited-edition toast rack, bronze cutlery) may be fragile or require handcare; choose accordingly.
- Shipping and returns: Look for UK postage, tracked delivery, and clear return windows. If the seller lists "brand new in box," make sure the box photos back that up.
- Cleaning practicalities: Novelty mugs with small parts are best handwashed; flatware sets might be labelled dishwasher-safe — follow manufacturer guidance.
