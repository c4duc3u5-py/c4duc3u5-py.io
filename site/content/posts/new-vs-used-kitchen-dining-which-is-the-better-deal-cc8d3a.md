## What to look for when you buy kitchen & dining items on eBay UK

- Condition and photos: For used items, ask for close-ups of joins, rims, gilding, and backing stamps. Look for hairline cracks and repair evidence.  
- Box presence: "With box" or "BNIB" raises resale value and protects fragile items in transit.  
- Material labels: Prefer 18/10 or 18/0 stainless for cutlery; porcelain/fine china for cups; ceramic for novelty mugs. If the seller doesn’t state material, ask.  
- Care instructions: Gilded rims and transfer-printed patterns can be damaged in the dishwasher—check before assuming dishwasher-safe.  
- Measurements: Cups, saucers and racks should have dimensions listed if you need stackable storage or a specific fit. Ask for diameters and heights.  
- Seller feedback and returns: Aim for >98% positive feedback and clear return policies if the item arrives damaged.

## Care tips and sizing guidance

- Hand-wash vintage or gilt-trimmed pieces to preserve finish. Use mild detergent and a soft cloth.  
- For cutlery, avoid prolonged contact with acidic foods; dry immediately after dishwashing to prevent spotting.  
- Novelty mugs with movable parts (like the Mr Potato Head) should be hand-washed, and removable parts stored separately when microwaving.  
- When using a spray gun: thin paint to manufacturer spec, strain before filling, and clean immediately after use to prevent nozzle blockages.
