## What to look for when buying tech & electronics under £80

- Compatibility first: Check phone dimensions for mounts and gimbals; verify laptop width and weight for stands. A small mismatch is the most common regret.
- Materials matter: Aluminium and reinforced plastics last longer than cheap thin plastic. For outdoor items, look for water-resistant fabric and heat-sealed seams.
- Weight and balance: For gimbals, weight distribution affects battery life and motor strain. For stands, heavier aluminium provides better stability than flimsy plastic.
- Condition and returns: "Brand new" is straightforward. For "opened – never used" or "pre-owned", ask the seller about returns, battery cycles (for electronic items), included accessories, and whether the original box/manual are present.
- Firmware and serial checks for branded kit: For DJI gear, confirm the model and ask the seller to show the device powering up and the serial number if you’re unsure about authenticity.
- Test before committing: If possible, arrange a local collection or request a short demo video showing the product working. For bike mounts, photos of the mount installed on a comparable frame are helpful.
- Shipping and total cost: Check postage costs and delivery times on the eBay listing before purchase — a cheap item can become pricey with fast postage or international delivery.

## Care, maintenance and small upgrades that extend lifespan

- Keep moving parts free of grit. A little silicon spray on hinges and pivot points (not on electronics) prevents creak and wear.
- Replace consumables: rubber pads, adhesive feet, and Velcro will wear out before the main product. Keep spare pads for laptop stands and extra Velcro straps for bike mounts.
- Store electronics charged at around 40–60% if you won’t use them for months — this helps lithium batteries retain capacity.
- Firmware updates: For items like the DJI Osmo Mobile 4, update firmware via the official app to benefit from bug fixes and new features.
