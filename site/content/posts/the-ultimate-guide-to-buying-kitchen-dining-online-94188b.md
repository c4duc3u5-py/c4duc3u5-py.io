## What to look for when buying kitchen & dining items online

- Verify materials and markings: look for "stainless steel 18/10", "porcelain", or "solid bronze" in the listing photos. Ask for close-ups of any maker stamps.
- Inspect dimensions: ask for cup capacity, saucer diameter, or cutlery lengths. Flatware balance depends on handle length and weight.
- Condition specifics for used items: request macro photos of edges, joins, and any chips, dents or plating wear. “Very Good” can still have light surface marks.
- Cleaning and care instructions: vintage metals often need hand washing; printed ceramics may be hand-wash only.
- Shipping and returns: check postage cost and estimated delivery time. For fragile ceramics, confirm the seller uses protective packaging and offers a return window.
- Seller reputation: prefer sellers with 98%+ positive feedback and clear return policies for higher-value items (like the £139.99 bronze set).
- Safety and compatibility: for tools like an HVLP spray gun, ensure it meets your air supply requirements and you have PPE and solvent compatibility.
