## What to look for when buying tech & electronics online (practical buying tips)
- Seller condition accuracy: verify condition descriptions like "New (other)" or "Opened – never used" against photos and the seller’s return policy. Ask questions if images are missing.  
- Shipping and returns: confirm shipping cost and delivery window — cheap items can become expensive with high postage. Check the return window (14–30 days typical) and who pays return postage.  
- Compatibility and dimensions: for physical gear (stand, phone mount), check dimensions, strap widths, and laptop size compatibility. A 15–16-inch laptop fits most stands, but always confirm listed max size/weight.  
- Materials and durability: look for aluminium, reinforced stitching, quality zips, and TPU touch windows. These small details predict longevity.  
- Firmware and security (routers): update firmware and change admin defaults. If the router was opened, ask if it was tested and whether it includes original leads and filters.  
- Pictures and reviews: prioritise listings with multiple clear images and buyer feedback on the seller. Images that show defects, packaging, or serial labels are more trustworthy.

## What to avoid and small things that matter
- Avoid sellers with poor feedback or no returns on used or opened items, especially for network hardware.  
- Don’t assume microwave or dishwasher safety for novelty ceramics; ask if it’s not stated.  
- Avoid overly cheap mounts without reinforced straps; they slip.  
- If you need reliable Wi‑Fi for work, skip low-end used routers unless you can confirm firmware and full functionality.

## Quick care & maintenance checklist
- Ceramics: handwash, avoid sudden temperature changes, keep small parts secured.  
- Fabric/TPU bike mounts: clean with mild soap, dry fully to prevent mildew.  
- Aluminium stand: wipe with a soft cloth; avoid liquids pooling near rubber feet.  
- Routers: keep in open air, update firmware, change passwords, and back up settings if possible.
