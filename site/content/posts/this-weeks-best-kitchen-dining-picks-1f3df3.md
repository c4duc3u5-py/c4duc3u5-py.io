## What to look for when you buy kitchen & dining items on eBay UK

- Condition accuracy: check the seller photos for wear, scratches or missing pieces. "Brand New" and "New in Box" are preferable if you need a pristine gift.  
- Materials matter: stainless steel grades, bone china, bronze and ceramic each require different care. Confirm material in the listing description.  
- Shipping and returns: the listings above note shipping is calculated at checkout — confirm dispatch origin and estimated delivery before you buy. Return windows vary by seller.  
- Boxed vs loose: boxed items retain value and are better for gifting. For collectibles, original packaging can double resale prices.  
- Function vs display: heavy bronze or limited-edition items may be better as display pieces — check whether the metal is food-safe if you plan to use it for eating.

## Quick care and sizing tips

- Cutlery: hand-dry stainless steel to avoid watermarks. For bronze or plated pieces, avoid dishwashers and acidic foods that can cause discoloration.  
- Ceramics & novelty mugs: handwash if the listing doesn’t confirm dishwasher safety; removable parts (like Mr Potato Head pieces) can deform in high heat.  
- Porcelain and bone china: stack with felt or paper between pieces if storing to prevent chipping.  
- Tools and hardware: spray guns must be cleaned immediately; store in a dry place to avoid internal corrosion.
